Geography Notes

1. Introduction to Geography

Geography is the study of Earth's physical features, climate, and human activities. It is divided into physical geography, human geography, and environmental geography.

2. Earths Structure

The Earth consists of the crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core. The lithosphere is the rigid outer layer, while the asthenosphere is a semi-molten layer beneath it.

3. Landforms

Mountains, plateaus, plains, valleys, and deserts form due to geological processes like erosion, weathering, and plate tectonics.

4. Climate and Weather

Climate is the long-term atmospheric condition, while weather refers to short-term changes. Climate zones include tropical, temperate, and polar regions.

5. Water Bodies

Oceans, seas, lakes, rivers, and groundwater play vital roles in the hydrological cycle, which includes evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.

6. Natural Disasters

Earthquakes, volcanoes, tsunamis, hurricanes, and floods result from natural processes. Disaster management strategies aim to reduce risks.

7. Population Geography

Demographics study population density, migration, birth rates, and death rates. Urbanization leads to city expansion and economic growth.

8. Economic Geography

It examines industries, agriculture, trade, and natural resources. Different economic activities include primary (farming, fishing), secondary (manufacturing), and tertiary (services).

9. Environmental Geography

Focuses on ecosystems, deforestation, pollution, and conservation. Climate change and global warming are major concerns.

10. Geographic Technologies

Modern geography uses GIS (Geographic Information Systems) and remote sensing for mapping and analyzing spatial data.
