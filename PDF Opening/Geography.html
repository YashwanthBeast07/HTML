Civics Notes

1. Introduction to Civics

Civics is the study of the rights and responsibilities of citizens and the functioning of government. It helps in understanding democracy, governance, and political institutions.

2. Forms of Government

Governments can be democratic, authoritarian, or monarchy-based. Democracy allows citizen participation, while authoritarian regimes concentrate power in one authority.

3. Constitution and Fundamental Rights

A constitution is a set of rules governing a country. Fundamental rights include equality, freedom, protection against exploitation, and cultural rights.

4. Legislature, Executive, and Judiciary

Legislature: Makes laws (e.g., Parliament).

Executive: Implements laws (e.g., President, Prime Minister).

Judiciary: Ensures justice and interprets laws.

5. Elections and Voting

Elections allow citizens to choose representatives. Free and fair elections ensure democratic governance.

6. Local Government

Municipalities and panchayats manage local administration, including public services, sanitation, and infrastructure.

7. Public Administration

Government agencies manage public welfare programs, law enforcement, and policymaking.

8. Human Rights and Duties

Human rights protect individuals against discrimination. Civic duties include obeying laws, paying taxes, and voting.

9. International Organizations

UN, WTO, WHO, and IMF influence global governance, human rights, and economic policies.

10. Social Justice and Equality

Ensures equal opportunities and eliminates discrimination based on caste, gender, or race.
