Physics Notes

Newton's Laws of Motion

First Law: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

Second Law: The acceleration of an object depends on the mass of the object and the force applied (F = ma).

Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Work, Energy, and Power

Work: Work is done when a force is applied to an object and the object moves in the direction of the force (W = Fd cosθ).

Energy: The capacity to do work. It exists in various forms such as kinetic energy (KE = 1/2 mv²) and potential energy (PE = mgh).

Power: The rate of doing work (P = W/t).

Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them (F = G(m₁m₂/r²)).

Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic waves are waves that can travel through the vacuum of outer space. They include radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays.

Ohm's Law

The current flowing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance (V = IR).

Thermodynamics

First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or converted from one form to another.

Second Law: The entropy of an isolated system always increases over time.

Third Law: As temperature approaches absolute zero, the entropy of a system approaches a constant minimum.

Wave-Particle Duality

Light and matter exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties, as described by quantum mechanics.

Relativity

Special Relativity: The laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers, and the speed of light in a vacuum is constant.

General Relativity: Gravity is the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.

Quantum Mechanics

Describes the behavior of matter and energy on very small scales, where classical physics no longer applies.

Optics

The study of light, including reflection, refraction, diffraction, and interference.

Magnetism

Describes how materials respond to magnetic fields and the interaction of moving charges with magnetic forces.

Fluid Mechanics

Studies the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) in motion and at rest.

Sound Waves

Mechanical waves that propagate through a medium, characterized by frequency, wavelength, and amplitude.

Semiconductors

Materials that have electrical properties between conductors and insulators, essential for modern electronics.

Atomic Structure

Describes the composition of atoms, including electrons, protons, and neutrons.

Nuclear Physics

The study of atomic nuclei, their interactions, and nuclear reactions.

Radioactivity

The process by which unstable atomic nuclei lose energy by emitting radiation.

Superconductivity

A phenomenon where certain materials exhibit zero electrical resistance at low temperatures.

Plasma Physics

The study of ionized gases, which are found in stars and fusion reactors.
